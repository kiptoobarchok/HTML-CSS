An audit report by KPMG on voter registration has revealed a decline in the number of registered youths for the forthcoming general election. IEBC chairman Wafula Chebukati said the audit shows the number of registered youths aged 18-34 years old stands at 39.84 per cent, representing a 5.27 per cent decline compared to 2017. "A further analysis of youth enrollment statistics shows a decline in the number of registered female voters by 7.75 percent between 2017 and 2022," Chebukati said.
